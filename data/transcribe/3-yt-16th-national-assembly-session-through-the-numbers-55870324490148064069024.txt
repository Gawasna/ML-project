The first session of Vietnam's 16th National Assembly was defined not just by its scale, but by what the numbers reveal about its significance. Across 12 working days, lawmakers made decisions that would shape the country's political and economic direction for the next phase of breakthrough development.
The National Assembly approved 39 senior leadership appointments, confirmed the election of 500 National Assembly deputies and more than 74,000 People's Council deputies nationwide, and passed nine laws alongside 31 resolutions, many with unanimous support. Deputies also mapped out Vietnam's core five-year socioeconomic and financial strategies, including plans for public investment, debt management, and national development priorities.
