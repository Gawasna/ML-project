 Now, just looking at the headlines, obviously we have to talk about the activities celebrating the 51st anniversary of the liberation of the South and national reunification. And to commemorate the occasion, Party General Secretary and State President Do Lam attended a flag-raising ceremony on Wednesday morning in Guangxi Province at the Hien Leung Bien Hai Special National Historical Site.

 And the red flag with a yellow star was raised to the top of the flag tower, a sacred symbol of independence, freedom and the aspiration for peace. Party and state leaders expressed deep gratitude to previous generations, as well as to those who sacrificed their lives for the nation.

 Nguyen Luong Bien Hai once served as the temporary military line between North and South Vietnam under the 1954 Geneva Accords. During more than two decades of war, Quang Tri became a key stronghold, helping defend the North and directly support the Southern battlefield.
